## 7. Three Quality Checkpoints

These are the moments requiring explicit review and approval before work advances.

**Checkpoint 1 — After brief, before research**
Review the hypothesis tree. Confirm all branches are MECE and cover the decision.
Push back on any missing or overlapping branch before approving.
*Gate: hypothesis tree approved → Phase 1 begins*

**Checkpoint 2 — After pyramid research, before drafting**
Review the 4–6 themes. Confirm each is a structural observation with a specific
strategic implication. Fix any theme that is still a data point or pyramid-level summary.
*Gate: themes approved → Phase 3 begins*

**Checkpoint 3 — After claim-scrutinizer (Pass 2), before red-team**
Review every NEEDS EVIDENCE flag on a thesis-critical claim. Either find the evidence
or explicitly downgrade the claim to hypothesis. No open load-bearing flags advance.
*Gate: all thesis-critical claims resolved → Pass 3 begins*

## 8. Validation Standards

### Confidence Labeling
Every claim in the output is labeled inline:
- **[H] High:** 3+ independent Tier 1–2 sources converge
- **[M] Medium:** 2 sources align; some methodology limitations
- **[L] Low:** Single source or sources diverge — caveat stated explicitly in prose

### Triangulation Rule
All thesis-critical findings require 2–3 independent Tier 1–2 sources.
A single source — even Tier 1 — is insufficient for any claim that, if wrong,
would weaken the governing thesis.

### Data Gap Protocol
When research returns thin, conflicting, or unverifiable data on a thesis-critical
claim, flag it explicitly:
```
DATA GAP: "[Claim]" — [reason: single source / sources conflict /
no third-party validation]. Warrants [specific action] before treating as confirmed.
```
Do not paper over gaps with qualified language. The gap is material information.

### CRAAP Minimum Score
Tier 1 sources must score ≥18/25 on the CRAAP test before use as primary evidence.
