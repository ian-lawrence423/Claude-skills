## 1. Analytical Frame — How to Structure This Market

### The Two-Geography Frame

All Shopee / Sea Ltd research must maintain separate analytical tracks for SEA and
Brazil. These are structurally different markets with different competitive dynamics,
different consumer profiles, and different investment theses. Never aggregate them
into a single market narrative.

```
Southeast Asia (SEA)        ← Shopee is the structural leader (~52% GMV share)
                               Thesis: defend and monetize a won market
                               Primary risk: TikTok content commerce model

Brazil                      ← Shopee is a challenger at statistical parity with MELI
                               Thesis: close the logistics gap to unlock monetization
                               Primary risk: AOV gap and logistics execution
```

### The Core Flywheel — Evidence Base as of March 2026

This is the governing investment thesis. Every research section should connect back
to which flywheel stage it validates or challenges.

| Flywheel Stage | Evidence Base |
|---------------|---------------|
| 1. SPX CPO reduction | SPX CPO declining 6–23% YoY across Asia and Brazil every quarter since Q1 2024 |
| 2. Free shipping subsidies | CPO savings fund free shipping programs — #1 consumer reason to use Shopee in Brazil (~65% citation rate) |
| 3. GMV growth | J.P. Morgan estimates Shopee Brazil 2025 GMV ~BRL 70B; SEA 2025E ~$159B |
| 4. Seller ad monetization | Higher GMV → larger seller base → higher willingness-to-pay for advertising |
| 5. Take-rate expansion | March 2026 Brazil fee restructuring: 14% commission + fixed fee (vs. prior 20% flat) |
| 6. Back to #1 | Higher take-rates + lower CPO → more free shipping subsidies → competitive advantage reinforced |

**Flywheel caveat:** The flywheel logic is directionally sound but contains one
unproven link — Step 4 (seller ad monetization scaling). The advertising revenue
ceiling risk in Brazil (Section 7) is the primary analytical challenge to the
flywheel as currently constructed.

**Key Brazil insight from J.P. Morgan Dec 2025 survey (n~1,000, 95% CI):**
- Shopee and MELI are statistically indistinguishable as most-used and most-preferred
- User overlap is extremely high: 48.7% of Brazilian online shoppers use all three
  top platforms (Shopee, MELI, Amazon) — these are complementary, not substitutes
- Shopee exclusive users (10.8%) > MELI exclusive users (7.2%) — Shopee has converted
  a meaningful price-sensitive cohort that does not use MELI

### TikTok Shop: Threat Assessment (as of March 2026)

**SEA:** Headwind, not existential threat.
- Price advantage is subsidized, not structural (J.P. Morgan Mar 2026: TikTok's
  apparent price advantage is voucher-funded — strip vouchers and advantage disappears)
- No logistics moat: relies on 3P logistics (primarily Jinrui/J&T in Indonesia)
- SPX handles >50% of Shopee SEA orders — TikTok cannot replicate this in <5 years
- Category concentration: heavily in low-ticket impulse (clothing, cosmetics, snacks);
  Shopee has stronger depth in electronics and home goods
- MAU resilience confirmed: Shopee MAU share holding at 47–75% across all 7 key
  markets with no evidence of structural MAU loss

**Brazil:** TikTok Shop launched May 2025. GMV grew ~25× in first 3 months (~$1M
→ ~$25.7M), reaching ~$46M by August 2025. Growing the market, not taking Shopee
share at this stage. Same logistics dependency risk as SEA. Watch: whether TikTok's
algorithm-driven discovery becomes a structural shopping behavior shift.

**Temu Brazil:** ~9.9% web traffic share (April 2025) — Brazil's #2 most-visited
e-commerce platform behind MELI by traffic. Brazil's August 2024 import duties on
low-value cross-border imports are a double-edged sword for Shopee: hurts Chinese
cross-border sellers but also protects Shopee's local seller base.

## 4. Consumer Behavior — Key Data Points

### SEA Consumer Behavior

- **Mobile-first:** 75%+ of SEA e-commerce transactions are mobile
- **Live commerce:** 86% of Thai shoppers have watched live shopping content;
  Indonesia live commerce penetration deepening rapidly
- **Shipping sensitivity:** 47% of SEA consumers cite shipping costs as primary
  reason for cart abandonment — the most significant purchase barrier
- **Cross-border:** SEA cross-border e-commerce growing at 11.1% CAGR,
  reaching $45.4B in 2025; primary driver is Chinese-origin merchandise

### Brazil Consumer Behavior (J.P. Morgan Survey, Dec 2025 / Jan 2026)

**Platform usage drivers:**

| Attribute | Shopee | MELI | Strategic Implication |
|-----------|--------|------|----------------------|
| #1 Reason to Use | Free Shipping (~65%) | Fast Shipping (~68%) | Shopee wins on cost; MELI wins on speed — classic value vs. premium |
| #2 Reason to Use | Lower Prices (~60%) | Reliable Shipping (~65%) | Different consumer propositions — explains high overlap |
| #3 Reason to Use | Promotions/Coupons (~38–42%) | Free Shipping (~60%) | Shopee #1 on promotions across all peers |
| Key Barrier | Slow Delivery (~30%) | High Prices (~50%) | Shopee's speed gap; MELI's price gap — each other's primary weakness |

- Shopee: dominates low-ticket clothing (68%), low-ticket electronics (53%),

## 7. Competitive Moat Assessment

### SPX Express — Primary Moat

Four dimensions of structural durability:

**Capital intensity:** Multi-year infrastructure investment requiring sustained capex
and operational build-out. An organic competitor must replicate years of investment
before reaching cost parity. Estimated 5+ year replication horizon.

**Data advantage:** End-to-end order data from click-to-delivery across hundreds of
millions of transactions. Feeds AI-driven route optimization, demand forecasting,
and seller behavior analysis. Data compounds — each order makes the next one cheaper.

**Route density operating leverage:** Each new seller joining SPX and each new order
increases route density, reducing last-mile CPO. This is the most important flywheel
mechanism — density improvements are non-linear.

**Financial sustainability:** Shopee operates with negative working capital (buyers pay
before sellers; deposits float in Shopee's ecosystem). Structural cash generation
advantage vs. asset-light 3P logistics competitors.

**Moat durability rating:** STRONG in SEA (5+ years). BUILDING in Brazil (3–5 year
horizon, contingent on FBS rollout execution).

## 8. Brazil Structural Risks — AOV Gap and Monetization Ceiling

This is the most analytically important section for any investment thesis on Shopee.
The AOV gap is not a transient problem — it has structural and cyclical components
that must be separated clearly.

### The AOV Gap

- **Shopee Brazil AOV:** ~$9–10 USD (Maybank IBG, June 2025)
- **MELI Brazil AOV:** ~$27 USD (same source)
- **Gap:** 2.7–3× — persistent across all available data vintages, though partially narrowing

**Economic consequences of 2.7–3× AOV disadvantage:**
- Gross commission per order: ~$1.26–1.40 (Shopee) vs. ~$3.78–4.46 (MELI) at 14% rate
- Ad revenue per order: ~$0.15–0.25 (Shopee) vs. ~$0.50–0.60 (MELI) — structural
- Logistics cost as % of gross commission: higher for Shopee despite lower absolute CPO

### Income Demographics (Brazil)

Brazil's online shopper base is predominantly middle-income. Class C (BRL 3,816–
9,540/month household income) represents 36.8% of all Brazilian digital consumers.
Shopee's deliberate targeting of Classes C and D creates a demographic moat and a
monetization ceiling simultaneously:

| Income Class | % Shopee Base | % MELI Base | % Amazon Base |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| Below R$5.6k/month | 60% | 32% | 21% |

### Three AOV Risk Factors

| Risk Factor | Nature | Time Horizon | What to Monitor |
|------------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| AOV gap vs. MELI (~2.7–3×) | Partially structural / cyclical | Closes as FBS-enabled electronics penetration grows — gated by FC density | Quarterly AOV disclosure in Sea earnings; electronics GMV share in Brazil |
| Premium brand avoidance | Structural (brand safety + demographic mismatch) | Two conditions must resolve simultaneously: (1) counterfeit/brand safety, (2) demographic income upgrade | Tier-1 brand official stores on Shopee Brazil; counterfeit enforcement actions |
| Ad revenue ceiling below MELI | Derivative of above two risks | Moves in step with AOV gap and brand avoidance resolution | Shopee Brazil ad take-rate as % of GMV; gap vs. MELI Ads 2.1%+ benchmark |
| Demographic income ceiling | Cyclical — improves with Brazilian income growth | Brazil real wage growth (4–5% YoY) will upgrade buyer purchasing power | Brazil real wage growth; Shopee buyer cohort AOV by vintage year |

**Advertising revenue benchmark:** MELI's Mercado Ads reached 2.1% of GMV in Q4 2024
(+41% YoY; 88% FX-neutral). Shopee's global ad metrics are directionally encouraging
(ad-paying sellers +20%+; average ad spend per seller +45%+) but Brazil-specific ad
take-rate as % of GMV is not yet disclosed.

### Verdict on AOV Risk

The low-AOV demographic risk is real and material, but not existential. It is the
primary reason Shopee's Brazil P&L timeline is longer than the bull case suggests.
Three factors work against treating it as a permanent ceiling:
1. FBS rollout enabling faster delivery → electronics category penetration
2. Brazilian real wage growth upgrading buyer purchasing power (4–5% YoY)
3. Shopee VIP membership program (7M+ subscribers globally, doubling QoQ in 4Q25)
   driving cohort-level AOV expansion

## 12. Open Questions — Carry-Forward from Prior Research

**High priority — changes the thesis if wrong:**

1. **What is Shopee Brazil's actual ad take-rate as % of GMV, and what is the
   trajectory?** This is the single most important unresolved question for the
   monetization thesis. MELI's 2.1%+ benchmark is the target — how far is
   Shopee from it, and what is the realistic timeline?

2. **Has FBS in Brazil shown any early GMV uplift comparable to Malaysia's +39%?**
   Management has disclosed the Malaysia metrics but not Brazil-specific FBS outcomes.
   The entire 2H26 inflection thesis depends on FBS working in Brazil — the data
   does not yet exist to confirm it.

3. **What is Shopee Brazil's electronics GMV share, and is it growing?**
   The AOV gap closes primarily through electronics category penetration. Without
   electronics GMV share data by quarter, it is impossible to assess whether the
   flywheel is working on its most important lever.

4. **Is TikTok Shop's Brazil GMV growth (~25× in 3 months) sustainable, or is it
   subsidy-funded like SEA?** The SEA TikTok pattern (voucher-dependent price
   advantage) may not hold in Brazil where logistics infrastructure matters more.
   Primary research on TikTok Shop seller economics in Brazil would resolve this.

5. **What is the status of Shopee's locker network expansion in Brazil and SEA
   ex-Taiwan?** Taiwan locker data (30% CPO reduction) is the proof of concept.
   The timeline for Brazil and SEA expansion determines when the CPO improvement
   compounds from this source.

**Medium priority — material for specific deal contexts:**

6. What is Shopee VIP's contribution to basket size and repeat purchase rate by
   market? 7M+ subscribers globally with QoQ doubling suggests material cohort
   value — but per-subscriber economics are not disclosed.

7. What is the counterfeit/brand safety enforcement action rate on Shopee Brazil,
   and is it improving? Premium brand avoidance is the second structural risk —
   the resolution mechanism is enforcement, not consumer behavior change.

8. At what FC density does Shopee Brazil's SPX CPO advantage over MELI become
   self-sustaining without further capex? The current CPO gap (~$1.00/order
   in Shopee's favor) is impressive but the network is still being built —
   understanding the economics at scale matters for the long-term thesis.

   Brazil only, or both. The analytical frames are different.

2. **Competitor scope:** Confirm which competitors require coverage. The full
   competitor map includes Shopee, MELI, Amazon Brazil, TikTok Shop, Temu,
   Lazada, and emerging local players. Not all need full profiles in every report.

3. **Data vintage check:** All figures in this template are from research completed
   March 2026. Web search to verify and update any figure before citing — this
   market moves fast (quarterly earnings, survey data, infrastructure disclosures).

4. **Open questions:** Review Section 12 before finalizing any research output.
   If the research addresses any of those questions, update this template.

5. **Flywheel validation:** Every section of the research should explicitly connect
   back to which flywheel stage it validates or challenges. If a finding weakens
   a flywheel link, state that explicitly rather than treating it as an isolated risk.

6. **FX disclosure:** Every USD figure for Brazil must state the BRL/USD assumption.
   Never cite Brazil GMV in USD without the sensitivity table or at minimum a
   footnote on the FX rate used.
