# Decomposition Patterns

Use these as starting templates. Adapt to the company, but preserve causal logic.

## 1. Recurring software / subscription revenue

Revenue =
- beginning recurring base
- plus new logo contribution
- plus expansion
- minus contraction
- minus churn
- plus usage / overage
- plus services / other

### Useful sub-drivers

#### Beginning recurring base
- beginning customer count
- beginning ARR / MRR
- starting seat or usage base

#### New logo contribution
- qualified pipeline
- opportunities created
- win rate
- average contract value
- contract term
- implementation lag
- percent live in period
- in-period revenue realization

#### Expansion
- upsell rate
- cross-sell incidence
- seat growth
- usage expansion
- price uplift

#### Contraction / churn
- gross churn rate
- downgrade rate
- seat loss
- logo churn
- revenue churn

#### Usage / overage
- eligible base
- usage intensity
- over-threshold incidence
- premium rate
- customer mix

#### Services / other
- implementation projects
- onboarding fees
- partner fees
- one-time revenue

## 2. Marketplace / payments / transaction revenue

Revenue =
- transaction volume x take rate
- plus subscription or platform fees
- plus services / other

### Useful sub-drivers
- active merchants or customers
- transactions per customer
- average order value
- GMV / payment volume
- attach rate
- take rate
- dispute / refund leakage
- merchant or channel mix
- settlement timing

## 3. Services / project model

Revenue =
- billable headcount x utilization x billing rate
- plus milestone or project revenue
- minus write-downs and slippage

### Useful sub-drivers
- average billable FTE
- hours per FTE
- billable utilization
- realized billing rate
- discounting
- backlog conversion
- project start timing
- project completion timing

## 4. Product / industrial / distribution model

Revenue =
- units sold x average selling price
- adjusted for mix, returns, and timing

### Useful sub-drivers
- customer count
- units per customer
- reorder frequency
- volume by product
- ASP
- discounting
- rebate programs
- returns
- channel mix
- geography mix

## 5. Gross profit

Gross profit =
- revenue
- minus cost of goods sold
- minus fulfillment / service delivery losses where appropriate

### Useful sub-drivers
- revenue bridge
- unit cost
- labor efficiency
- procurement cost
- freight / logistics
- returns / claims / warranty
- product or customer mix
- utilization / fixed cost absorption

## 6. EBITDA

EBITDA =
- gross profit
- minus sales and marketing
- minus G&A
- minus R&D or product
- adjusted for one-time items where appropriate

### Useful sub-drivers
- headcount by function
- compensation per FTE
- contractor spend
- fixed vs variable spend
- sales productivity
- support ratio
- overhead absorption
- one-time spend

## 7. Cash flow

Cash flow =
- EBITDA
- plus or minus working capital movement
- minus capex
- minus cash exceptional items
- plus or minus financing items if relevant to the user's framing

### Useful sub-drivers
- DSO
- DPO
- inventory days
- deferred revenue movement
- prepaid movement
- capex timing
- tax cash timing
- one-time legal or restructuring cash items

## 8. Practical rules

### Rule A: split hybrid businesses first
If the company has multiple revenue engines, create separate trees first:
- recurring software
- services
- transaction
- hardware
- partner or commission revenue

Then roll them into one top-line tree.

### Rule B: separate realization from contract creation
Do not let bookings and recognized revenue live in the same node.

### Rule C: break averages into components where possible
If an average hides mix or behavior, decompose it:
- ASP -> list price, discount rate, product mix
- retention -> logo churn, contraction, expansion
- productivity -> volume, conversion, rate, utilization

### Rule D: keep branches MECE
Each branch should be mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive enough to explain the total.
